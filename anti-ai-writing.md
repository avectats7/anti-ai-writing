# Anti-AI Writing Rules

These rules apply to every single piece of writing produced for or by Tats. No exceptions. The goal is writing that sounds like a sharp, experienced human — not a language model trying to sound like one.

---

## The Core Rule

If a sentence could have been written by ChatGPT, rewrite it. If a word appears on the banned list below, replace it. If a structure matches the patterns below, break it.

## Banned Phrases — Never Use These

### Sycophantic Openers
Never open with praise for the question or topic.

- "Great question!"
- "Absolutely!"
- "Certainly!"
- "Of course!"
- "Sure!"
- "That's a really interesting point."
- "I'd be happy to help with that."

### Summary and Conclusion Phrases
Never signal the end of writing like an AI completing a task.

- "In conclusion,"
- "In summary,"
- "To sum up,"
- "All in all,"
- "At the end of the day,"
- "Ultimately,"
- "To summarise,"
- "The bottom line is,"

### Hedging and Qualifier Phrases
These add length without adding meaning. Cut them.

- "It is worth noting that"
- "It is important to note that"
- "It's worth mentioning that"
- "It should be noted that"
- "Needless to say,"
- "As you may already know,"
- "It goes without saying,"

### Vague Opening Phrases
These stall the actual point. Get to the point instead.

- "In today's world,"
- "In today's fast-paced world,"
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of,"
- "In the age of,"
- "At the intersection of,"
- "In recent years,"
- "Now more than ever,"
- "First and foremost,"
- "Here's the kicker:"

### Buzzword Phrases
Corporate fluff. Never use.

- "Unlock the potential of"
- "Unleash the power of"
- "Delve into the world of"
- "Pave the way for"
- "At the forefront of"
- "Harness the power of"
- "Embark on a journey"
- "Push the boundaries of"
- "A gateway to"
- "Bridging the gap between"
- "Lay the groundwork for"
- "Foster a culture of"
- "Navigate the complexities of"
- "Spearhead the initiative"
- "Capitalize on the opportunity"
- "Drive meaningful impact"
- "Create lasting change"
- "Deliver value"
- "Move the needle"
- "Take it to the next level"

### Contrastive AI Patterns
These are formulaic AI sentence structures. Avoid them.

- "It's not X, it's Y." *(e.g. "It's not a product, it's a movement.")*
- "While X is important, Y is even more crucial."
- "Not just X, but Y." *(overused for emphasis)*

## Banned Structural Patterns

### Bullet Points With Bolded Titles
This is the single most common AI formatting tell. Pattern looks like:

> **Key benefit:** This feature helps users accomplish X.

The bold title is almost always just a restatement of what follows. Write in prose instead.

### The Rule of Three
LLMs default to grouping things in threes. "Fast, simple, and effective." "Growth, retention, and conversion." It becomes a pattern. Vary sentence structure. Sometimes use two. Sometimes four. Sometimes none.

### Dashes and Hyphens: Forbidden
Em dashes (—), en dashes (–), and hyphens (-) are all forbidden in copy. No exceptions.

Em dashes and en dashes are a known AI writing tell and make prose feel like it was assembled by a machine. Replace them with a full stop, a colon, or rewrite the sentence so the connector is not needed.

Hyphens in compound words are also forbidden. Write around them. "Go-to-market" becomes "GTM" or "go to market." "Pre-launch" becomes "before launch." "Mid-raise" becomes "mid raise" or reword entirely. If a term is so established it cannot be rewritten (a proper name, an acronym), use the shortest accepted form.

The one exception: hyphens within acronyms that are standard industry usage (e.g. B2B, B2C) are acceptable because they are not punctuation — they are part of the term itself.

### Excessive Bullet Points
When everything becomes a list, nothing gets communicated clearly. Prefer prose. Reserve bullets for genuinely list-like things: steps, items, comparisons. Not for ideas that belong in a sentence.

### Weak Verb Substitutions
AI avoids strong, direct verbs. Watch for these and replace them:

| Weak AI Construction | Replace With |
|---|---|
| "serves as a" | use a strong verb directly |
| "acts as a" | use a strong verb directly |
| "marks the" | use a strong verb directly |
| "features" (instead of has) | "has" |
| "offers" (as filler) | "has" or cut entirely |
| "utilize" | "use" |
| "facilitate" | "help" or "make" |
| "endeavour" (as filler) | cut or rewrite |

### Formulaic Transitions
AI uses these to stitch paragraphs together mechanically. They should almost always be cut or replaced with a sentence that genuinely connects ideas.

- Furthermore,
- Moreover,
- Additionally, *(doubly banned — also on word list)*
- Subsequently,
- Accordingly,
- In addition,
- As previously mentioned,
- As noted above,
- With that in mind,

## What to Do Instead

- **Use short sentences.** They read faster and hit harder.
- **Lead with the point.** Don't build to it.
- **Use specific, concrete language.** "Revenue grew 3x" beats "significant growth was achieved."
- **Write like you're talking to someone smart.** Skip the scaffolding.
- **Use strong, direct verbs.** "Cut costs" not "facilitate cost reduction."
- **Have a point of view.** AI hedges. Humans take a stance.
- **Vary sentence length.** Long sentences followed by short ones create rhythm. It also keeps people reading.
- **Read it out loud.** If it sounds like a press release or a LinkedIn post written by a committee, rewrite it.
