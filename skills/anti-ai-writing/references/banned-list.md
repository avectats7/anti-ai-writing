## The core rule

If a sentence could have been written by ChatGPT, rewrite it.

If a word appears on the banned list, replace it with the natural equivalent.

If a structure matches the banned patterns, break it.

There are edge cases where a banned word has a legitimate technical use (e.g. "framework" for a named software framework). The rule bans the word as filler, not as a precise term. When in doubt, ask: is this word doing real work, or is it a fingerprint? If the sentence reads the same after deletion, the word was a fingerprint.

Additionally. Boasts. Bolstered. Crucial. Delve. Delve into. Emphasizing. Enduring. Garner. Intricate. Intricacies. Interplay. Key (as adjective, e.g. "a key factor"). ### Era 3 (mid-2025 onward)

## Banned phrases

### Sycophantic openers
Never open by praising the question, the topic, or the user.

"Great question." "Absolutely." "Certainly." "Of course." "Sure." "That's a really interesting point." "I'd be happy to help with that."

### Summary and conclusion phrases
Never signal the end of a piece of writing the way a chatbot completes a task.

"In conclusion." "In summary." "To sum up." "All in all." "At the end of the day." "Ultimately." "To summarise." "The bottom line is."

### Hedging and qualifier phrases
These add length without adding meaning.

"It is worth noting that." "It is important to note that." "It's worth mentioning that." "It should be noted that." "Needless to say." "As you may already know." "It goes without saying."

### Vague opening phrases
These delay the actual point.

"In today's world." "In today's fast-paced world." "In the ever-evolving landscape of." "In the age of." "At the intersection of." "In recent years." "Now more than ever." "First and foremost." "Here's the kicker."

### Buzzword phrases
Corporate filler. None of them carry real meaning.

"Unlock the potential of." "Unleash the power of." "Delve into the world of." "Pave the way for." "At the forefront of." "Harness the power of." "Embark on a journey." "Push the boundaries of." "A gateway to." "Bridging the gap between." "Lay the groundwork for." "Foster a culture of." "Navigate the complexities of." "Spearhead the initiative." "Capitalize on the opportunity." "Drive meaningful impact." "Create lasting change." "Deliver value." "Move the needle." "Take it to the next level."

### Contrastive AI patterns
Formulaic sentence structures that became a recognisable tell.

"It's not X, it's Y." (e.g. "It's not a product, it's a movement.")
"While X is important, Y is even more crucial."
"Not just X, but Y." (overused for emphasis)

## Banned structural patterns

### Bullet points with bolded titles
The single most common AI formatting tell.

Pattern: "**Key benefit:** This feature helps users accomplish X."

The bold title almost always restates what follows. Write in prose instead. If a list is genuinely a list (steps, items to compare), use plain bullets without bolded titles, or use a numbered list.

### The default rule of three
LLMs reach for triads by reflex. "Fast, simple, and effective." "Growth, retention, and conversion." "Plan, execute, and deliver."

The fix: vary the count. Sometimes two items. Sometimes four. Sometimes none.

If you do use three items, make sure each adds something the other two do not. Three interchangeable items is a tell. Three items where each does specific work is fine.

### Em dashes, en dashes, and standalone hyphens
All forbidden in copy. No exceptions.

Em dash (—) and en dash (–) are a known AI tell. They make prose feel assembled rather than written. Replace with a full stop, a colon, or rewrite the sentence so the connector is unnecessary.

Standalone hyphens used as punctuation (e.g. "the report - which arrived late - was wrong") are also banned. Rewrite as parentheses, commas, or a separate sentence.

Hyphens in compound words are also banned. Write around them. "Go-to-market" becomes "GTM" or "go to market." "Pre-launch" becomes "before launch." "Mid-raise" becomes "mid raise" or reword. Exception: hyphens that are part of a standard acronym (B2B, B2C) are fine because the hyphen is part of the term, not punctuation.

### Excessive bullet points
When everything is a list, nothing is communicated. Prefer prose. Reserve bullets for content that is genuinely list-shaped: steps, items, comparisons, options.

### Formulaic transitions
AI uses these to stitch paragraphs together mechanically. They almost always should be cut or replaced with a sentence that genuinely connects the ideas.

Furthermore. Moreover. Additionally. Subsequently. Accordingly. In addition. As previously mentioned. As noted above. With that in mind.

If two paragraphs do not connect without a transition word, the connection is weak. Fix the connection, do not paper it with a transition.

## Weak verb constructions

AI avoids direct verbs. Watch for these and replace them with the direct form.

| Weak construction | Replace with |
|---|---|
| "serves as a" | a strong verb directly |
| "acts as a" | a strong verb directly |
| "marks the" | a strong verb directly |
| "features" (as filler) | "has" |
| "offers" (as filler) | "has" or cut entirely |
| "utilize" | "use" |
| "facilitate" | "help" or "make" |
| "endeavour" (as filler) | cut or rewrite |
| "is positioned to" | "will" or cut |
| "is designed to" | "will" or rewrite as direct claim |

## Spanish AI tells (a starter map)

The default AI voice in Spanish has its own fingerprints. This list is not exhaustive but covers the most common.

### Banned words in Spanish

Adentrarse en. Aprovechar (as filler equivalent of "leverage"). Aprovechemos. Brindar (overused for "give"). Crucial. Desarrollar (often filler). Diversa. Diversos. Dinámico. Empoderar. Enriquecer. Esencial (as filler). Estimular. Excepcional (as filler). Explorar (as filler). Fomentar. Fortalecer. Fundamental (as filler). Holístico. Imprescindible (as filler). Innovador (as filler). Integrar (as filler). Interactuar. Maximizar. Multifacético. Navegar (when not literal). Notable. Optimizar. Panorama (e.g. "panorama actual"). Paradigma. Permitirá. Potenciar. Potencial. Relevante (as filler). Revolucionario. Robusto. Sinergia. Sólido. Subrayar. Transformar (as filler).

### Banned Spanish phrases

"En la actualidad" (vague opener).
"En el mundo actual."
"En el competitivo mundo de."
"Hoy en día más que nunca."
"Cabe destacar que."
"Vale la pena mencionar que."
"No solo X, sino también Y."
"Marcar la diferencia."
"Llevar al siguiente nivel."
"Aprovechar al máximo."
"Potenciar al máximo."
"Liberar el potencial de."

### Banned structures in Spanish
Same as English: em dashes, the default rule of three, bulleted bold-title lists, formulaic transitions ("Además," "Por otra parte," "Cabe mencionar").

The Spanish list is less comprehensive than the English list above because Spanish-language AI tells have been studied less. When in doubt about a Spanish word, apply the same test: does it carry real meaning, or is it filler? Cut filler.
